v5.1 Scoring Model — How It Works

Eight independent scoring layers evaluate every PDUFA event before the FDA decision date. Each layer uses publicly available data — FDA approval history, ClinicalTrials.gov enrollment data, SEC EDGAR filings, and regulatory pathway designations.

Scoring Layers

1. Indication Base Rate — Historical FDA approval rates by therapeutic area (2014–2024 NME+BLA data). Oncology: 85%. Rare/Orphan: 86.2%. CNS/Neurology: 49%. Psychiatry: 49%. Cardiovascular: 65%.

2. Regulatory Pathway — BLA, NDA, sNDA, ANDA modifiers. Supplements carry lower incremental value.

3. Designation Modifiers — Breakthrough (+8%), Fast Track (+3%), Priority Review (+4%), Accelerated Approval (+5%), Orphan (+4%). Cumulative.

4. AdCom Analysis — Vote ratios. Near-unanimous (≥90%): +6%. Narrow positive (60–75%): −2%. Negative: −10%.

5. Prior CRL Modifier — Clinical CRL: ×0.75. Manufacturing CRL: ×0.90. Largest penalty in the model.

6. STN Risk — Predicts whether an approval moves the stock. Factors: PoA priced-in, app type, cap, resubmission, competition, first-in-class.

7. Dilution Risk — SEC EDGAR shelf/ATM monitoring. Reduces Net Edge score.

8. ClinicalTrials.gov — Enrollment count, site count, completion status. Validates pivotal data existence.

Accuracy Rules

Correct: PoA ≥ 65% and approved, OR PoA < 50% and CRL. Delayed events excluded. STN measured on approvals only.

Updated within 24 hours of every PDUFA resolution. No exceptions. No deletions.

Where the Model Fails
Manufacturing CRLs on strong clinical packages — INSM (Broji) had orphan + priority review + strong Phase 3. Scored 76.6%. CRL was entirely manufacturing-driven. The model weights clinical data heavily and underweights CMC risk for drugs without prior CRL history.

Oncology efficacy misreads — RVMD and ALKS scored above 78% because Oncology base = 85% plus breakthrough designation. Both received CRLs because Phase 3 data missed primary endpoints — information the model cannot capture from pre-decision flags alone.

sNDA underscoring — IDYA scored 48% because Psychiatry base (49%) + sNDA penalty (-5%). sNDA supplements for existing approved drugs have higher approval rates than novel NDAs. Calibration fix tracked for v5.2.
