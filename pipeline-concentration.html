Concentration Scoring Methodology
Concentration Risk Score (0–100, higher = more concentrated = more risk)

Pipeline Depth (40 pts) — 1 drug = 40, 2 drugs = 25, 3 drugs = 12, 4+ drugs = 0
Revenue Dependency (25 pts) — Pre-revenue = 25, <$50M = 18, $50-200M = 10, $200M-1B = 3, $1B+ = 0
Approval History (15 pts) — No approved drugs = 15, 1 approved = 8, 2+ approved = 0. Inferred from revenue when not explicit.
Market Cap Floor (10 pts) — Sub-$500M = 10, $500M-$2B = 5, $2B+ = 0
Cash Runway Risk (10 pts) — <12 months = 10, 12-24 = 5, 24+ = 0
Commercial-stage discount — $1B+ revenue + $5B+ cap = -15pts. $500M+ rev + $2B+ cap = -10pts.
Mega-pharma bypass — $50B+ cap + $5B+ revenue = auto LOW (10). Individual PDUFAs are label expansions, not existential.

EXTREME (≥70): Single-pipeline, pre-revenue, micro-cap. Full position risk on one PDUFA.
HIGH (50-69): Limited pipeline, minimal revenue. Significant binary exposure.
MODERATE (30-49): Some diversification through multiple assets or revenue.
LOW (<30): Well-diversified pipeline with revenue base. PDUFA outcome is meaningful but not existential.
⚠️ Pipeline concentration is a risk classification, not a directional recommendation. High concentration does not mean a stock will fail — it means the outcome is more binary. Position sizing decisions are solely your own.
