The Method

Not a calendar. Not a screener.vs BioPharma Catalyst
An intelligence platform.

Eight independent analysis layers sourced from FDA filings, SEC EDGAR, and peer-reviewed literature (Hay et al. 2014 · Wong et al. 2019). Every score is documented. No black boxes. Unlike calendar-only tools, Submarine Catalyst models both approval probability and post-decision price behavior from the same dataset.

01
Calendar Ingestion

Pulls the live PDUFA calendar daily. Every upcoming FDA decision — small-cap, mid-cap, and large-cap — across the entire biotech universe gets queued for scoring automatically.

02
PoA Computation

Blends indication-specific base rates (Hay 2014, Wong 2019) with review pathway type, AdCom vote parsing, and SEC 8-K filing language signals to produce a calibrated approval probability.

03
Sell-The-News Risk

Approval ≠ stock move. The STN model scores whether approval is priced in, revenue potential, commercial launch burden, and institutional positioning. Calibrated against real outcomes — RCKT (approved, −20%), VNDA (approved, +45%), TVTX (approved, +33.29%). STN HIGH was also flagged on REPL before the 2nd CRL — stock dropped 64% regardless of the reason.

04
Dilution Radar

Scans SEC EDGAR for 424B5 filings, S-3 shelf registrations, ATM offerings, and sales agent changes near PDUFA dates. Detects the capital raises that destroy post-approval gains before you buy.

05
Net Edge Score

Combines PoA, STN Risk, Dilution Radar, cash runway, and insider activity into a single composite. One number that answers "is this a good trade" — not just "will it get approved."

06
Behavioral Patterns

Classifies each event into five post-catalyst behaviors: Post-Approval Spike, Deep Dive, Strong Ascend, Buyout Wave, or Surface-Level Reaction. Not predictions — behavioral tendencies drawn from historical data.

07
TAM / Market Cap Ratio

Estimates the drug's Total Addressable Market and compares it to the company's market cap. A $500M company with a $10B drug market has 20x upside. A $2B company with a $200M drug market has limited room. TAM/Cap tells you if the stock can actually move.

08
Buyout Probability

Scores acquisition likelihood from 8 factors: asset quality (approved + orphan + breakthrough), acquirer urgency from a 14-company Big Pharma patent cliff database (ABBV, MRK, PFE, LLY, BIIB and 9 others), strategic cluster, insider momentum, valuation gap, balance sheet, and penalty filters for litigation and management resistance.

Who Built This

A submarine, a spreadsheet, and a problem nobody was solving.

I spent three years in the Navy, most of it on the USS Montana during dry dock in Newport News, Virginia. Eleven months inside a boat that wasn't in the water yet. What you learn in that environment is that everything runs on procedure, verification, and trusting your instruments when your instincts are screaming something else. That stuck with me.

I got medically separated and enrolled at Texas Tech for a BBA in IT systems. Somewhere between learning SQL and reading SEC filings for fun, I started trading biotech PDUFA events. The problem was obvious pretty fast. I could spend five hours researching a single ticker, digging through FDA history, EDGAR filings, clinical trial data, cash runway math, and still miss something that tanked the trade. So I started building a tool to do the research for me.

That tool became Submarine Catalyst. It started as a Python script on my laptop. Now it runs eight analysis layers across 870+ biotech companies, updated daily by an automated pipeline that costs me less than $30 a month to operate. I'm 23, I'm still in school, and I built this because I needed it. Turns out other people need it too.

The RCKT event is what made this real. The model said 77.5% chance of approval. It got approved. The stock dropped 20%. I posted the loss publicly and it got 21,000 views. That one outcome led to the Sell-The-News Risk layer, the Dilution Radar, and every feature that separates this platform from a basic PDUFA calendar.

REPL on April 10, 2026 was the only PoA miss in the live record. The model called 84% approval. FDA issued a 2nd CRL. The stock dropped 64%. The STN HIGH flag had correctly identified severe downside before the decision — the risk layer was right even when the probability layer was wrong. That distinction matters. Every outcome gets logged, including the ones that hurt. That's the whole point.

TAM (Total Addressable Market) is the estimated annual peak revenue of the drug's indication. TAM/Cap ratio compares that market to the company's current market cap. A ratio of 10x means the drug market is 10 times the company's value — significant upside on approval. A ratio below 1x means the drug market is smaller than the company — limited upside even on good news. RCKT had a TAM/Cap ratio of 0.04x (gene therapy for ~30 patients). VNDA had 5x (gastroparesis, $1.5B market). The ratio predicted which approval was worth trading.
